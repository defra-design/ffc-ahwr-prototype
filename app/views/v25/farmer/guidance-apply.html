How to apply for livestock health and welfare review and endemic disease follow-ups

Cattle, sheep and pig farmers can get funding for a vet to test livestock for diseases and advise on improving animal welfare and productivity.

From: Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs and Rural Payments Agency

Applies to England

What the [scheme name] is

The [scheme name] forms part of the Animal Health and Welfare Pathway. The [scheme name] will provide payments to livestock keepers to fund livestock reviews by vets.

You can get funding for a vet or team chosen by a vet, to visit your farm and carry out a [scheme name] if you keep:

  • beef cattle
  • dairy cattle
  • sheep
  • pigs

Livestock health and welfare review

There are two parts to the [scheme name]. The first part is the livestock health and welfare review. You can choose to have just a livestock health and welfare review.

A livestock health and welfare review will help you:

  • test for specific endemic diseases in beef cattle, dairy cattle and pigs
  • the effectiveness of medicines to treat worms in sheep
  • increase livestock productivity
  • improve animal welfare

After the vet has completed a health and welfare review, you can choose to have an endemic disease follow-up, where the vet tests for key diseases on the same species as your review.

Endemic disease follow-up

The second part is the endemic disease follow up; this must follow a livestock health and welfare review.

An endemic disease follow-up will help you:

  • understand levels of key diseases and syndromes on farm through targeted testing
  • reduce key disease levels and improve disease and health status through bespoke vet advice and a detailed biosecurity assessment
  • increase livestock productivity
  • improve animal welfare

You can choose the vet that you want to do the review or endemic disease follow-up. The review and follow-up are not an inspection or audit.

This is a multiannual agreement which will allow you:

  • to have up to 3 reviews over the course of your agreement, for one livestock type, every 10 months
  • to then have up to 3 endemic disease follow-ups on the same herd or flock within 10 months of the review

Check you’re eligible to apply for [scheme name]

To be eligible to make an agreement and claim funding you must have a minimum number of animals present when the vet visits your farm to do the review or endemic disease follow-up.

You must have one of the following:

  • 11 or more beef cattle
  • 11 or more dairy cattle
  • 21 or more sheep
  • 51 or more pigs

The animals can be any age.

The livestock must be registered in England and you must be responsible for their welfare when the vet carries out the review or endemic disease follow-up visit. You can apply at any time of the year.

Your business must have:

  • a single business identifier (SBI) linked to a county parish holding (CPH) number registered in England
  • a CPH number

You can only apply for a review or endemic disease follow-up on a CPH registered in England.

The CPH number cannot be:

  • an 8,000 number used for abattoirs, pet food manufacturers, racing pigeon establishments and showgrounds
  • a 9,000 number used for poultry keepers

If you have received a lump sum payment to leave or retire from farming, you can still apply for a funding if you meet the qualifying criteria.

How much funding you could get for [scheme name]

You’ll get funding for each livestock type, not for each animal.
