Guidance

Farmers: get a funded annual health and welfare review of your livestock

Find out what to expect from a review, check if you’re eligible, the funding you’ll get, how to apply and claim.

You can get funding for a vet or team chosen by a vet, to visit your farm and carry out a health and welfare review if you keep:

  • beef cattle
  • dairy cattle
  • sheep
  • pigs

You can have a review of one eligible type of livestock. If you have more than one eligible type, you must choose the one you would like reviewed. You cannot apply for another review of the same herd or flock until 10 months after you submitted your last application.

A health and welfare review will help you:

  • reduce endemic diseases and conditions
  • increase animal productivity
  • improve animal welfare

The review is optional. It will not be an inspection or be used for compliance or monitoring.

You can choose the vet that you want to do the review.

What you need to do

  • 1. Check you’re eligible to apply.
  • 2. Apply for a review.
  • 3. Set up a review.
  • 4. Claim your funding.

Check you’re eligible to apply

You must have a minimum number of animals when the vet visits your farm to do the review. You must have one of the following:

  • 11 or more beef cattle
  • 11 or more dairy cattle
  • 21 or more sheep
  • 51 or more pigs

The livestock must be registered in England and you must be responsible for their welfare.

Your business must have a valid Single Business Identifier (SBI) that’s linked to an English County Parish Holding (CPH) number. The CPH number can be permanent or temporary but cannot be an 8000 or 9000 series CPH number.

If you’ve received the lump sum payment to leave or retire from farming you can still apply for a review.

How much funding you’ll get

You’ll get funding for each livestock type, not for each animal. You’ll get:

  • £522 for a beef cattle review
  • £372 for a dairy cattle review
  • £436 for a sheep review
  • £684 for a pig review

The funding amount for each type of livestock is different because:

  • testing may take longer for some types of livestock
  • some results cost more to analyse

This funding will cover the endemic disease or condition testing costs. It will contribute towards the costs for the vet’s time and your time.

What to expect from a review

There’s no set amount of time for a review. It’s likely to take between 2 to 3 hours of your time and the vet’s time. You may choose to ask the vet to spread it over several regular visits to the farm or do it all in one visit.

You must ask the vet to:

  • carry out endemic disease or condition testing of the livestock type you’re having reviewed
  • advise on the health and welfare of these livestock – including issues such as lameness, body conditioning or mobility scoring

You may also ask the vet to discuss one or both of the following as part of the review:

  • biosecurity, including internal and external biosecurity recommendations
  • medicine usage, including antibiotics, vaccinations and how you can register and submit data on the Agriculture and Horticulture Development Board (AHDB) Medicine Hub

You do not need to discuss both these topics. The review is flexible, focus on what is important for your farm when the vet visits you.

You should agree with the vet:

  • when they’ll carry out the review
  • which optional topics they’ll focus on when they visit

After the review you must ask the vet to give you a:

  • written report, including recommended follow-up actions and the relevant endemic disease or condition test results from the laboratory
  • review summary

Apply for a review

You can apply for an annual health and welfare review of your livestock.

Set up a review

Once you’ve accepted your agreement offer and received your agreement number, contact the vet to arrange a review.

What you need to ask the vet to do

Before the review ask the vet to:

  • read the guidance on how to carry out an annual health and welfare review of livestock
  • read the guidance on how to carry out disease and endemic condition testing and recommended laboratories for the review
  • confirm they’re able to do the review

What to ask the vet to test for

You must ask the vet to test for:

  • bovine viral diarrhoea (BVD) in beef cattle
  • bovine viral diarrhoea (BVD) in dairy cattle
  • the effectiveness of worming treatments in sheep
  • porcine reproductive and respiratory syndrome (PRRS) in pigs

You must ask them to test the minimum required number of animals for the type of review they’re doing. You should advise them to get samples analysed at one of the recommended laboratories.

To find out more about testing including the minimum required numbers and recommended laboratories, read the guidance on how to carry out disease and endemic condition testing.

You can:

  • use these review test results for other accreditation schemes you’re a member of
  • schedule your review to fit in with your existing testing schedule if you’re already testing for these diseases or conditions to take advantage of the funding

You cannot:

  • use other accreditation scheme test results for this review funding
  • ask the vet to test for other medical conditions or diseases as part of the review

Farm visit

The vet will visit the farm to carry out testing and discuss the health and welfare of your livestock. You may also choose to ask the vet to discuss biosecurity measures and medicine usage.

Written report

After the vet has carried out the review, you must ask them to give you a written report.

The written report must include:

  • endemic disease or condition test results from the laboratory
  • advice or suggested species-specific health and welfare actions
  • other review findings - for example, biosecurity recommendations and medicine usage
  • agreed achievable actions to address issues raised by the review or testing - likely to be 2 to 3 but you can base it on individual needs

The report will not be shared with Defra or used for site visits or other compliance checks. It can be digital or paper.

Get evidence the review has taken place

You must ask the vet to give you a summary of the review. This is evidence that the review took place. You must supply this evidence if the Rural Payments Agency (RPA) asks to see it.

The review summary must include:

  • the date of the vet’s last visit to the farm for the review
  • the type of livestock reviewed
  • how many animals were tested for endemic diseases or conditions – you must ask the vet to test a minimum number of animals for you to receive funding
  • the unique reference number for laboratory test results
  • the vet’s name and Royal College of Veterinary Surgeons (RCVS) number
  • confirmation the minimum number of animals were on the farm when the vet did the review
  • confirmation the vet has given you a written report

You must ask the vet to sign and date the summary. It can be digital or paper.

Claim funding for a review

You can claim your funding after the vet has carried out the review and given you:

  • a written report
  • a review summary

You’ll be asked to share the following information:

  • the date of the vet’s last visit to the farm for the review
  • vet’s name and Royal College of Veterinary Surgeons (RCVS) number
  • the unique reference number for laboratory test results

Claim funding for an annual health and welfare review of your livestock.

It’s your responsibility to pay the vet when the review is complete.

You must make sure the review takes place and you claim funding within 6 months of receiving your agreement number.

Get help with your application or claim

If you cannot apply or claim online or have a question about your application or claim, contact the Rural Payments Agency.

Have your agreement number with you when you call or include it in your email if you have it.

  • Rural Payments Agency
  • Telephone: 03000 200 301
  • Monday to Friday, 8:30am to 5pm (except public holidays)
  • ruralpayments@defra.gov.uk
