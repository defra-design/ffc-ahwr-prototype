Guidance

SFI annual health and welfare review

Farmers who keep cattle, sheep and pigs can get funding to pay for a vet or vet-led team to visit their farm and carry out an annual health and welfare view.

  • Contents
  • Check you’re eligible to apply
  • Register your interest
  • Apply for a review

To apply to get funding for a vet or team chosen by a vet, to visit your farm and carry out a health and welfare review you need to: 

  • check you’re eligible to apply 
  • register your interest

Check your eligible to apply

To be eligible to apply you must have a minimum number of animals when the vet visits your farm to do the review.

You must have one of the following:  

  • 11 or more beef cattle 
  • 11 or more dairy cattle 
  • 21 or more sheep 
  • 51 or more pigs  

The livestock must be registered in England and you must be responsible for their welfare. The business you’re applying for must have:

  • a valid single business identifier (SBI) number linked to a permanent or temporary English County Parish Holding (CPH) number
  • a CPH number that’s not an ‘8000’ series

    • If you’ve received the lump sum payment to leave or retire from farming you can still apply for a review.

    Register your interest

    When you register you’ll be asked to provide the following information to check you’re eligible to register:

    • your customer reference number (CRN)
    • the single business identifier (SBI) number of the business you want to apply for the review
    • the email associated to your Rural Payments account 

    After you’ve registered you’ll receive an email with information about the next steps. 

    You may have to wait a short time before you’re given access to the service depending on how many people register.  This will be explained in the email.
