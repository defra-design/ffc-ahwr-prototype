Guidance

How to apply for funding to improve animal health and welfare

Cattle, sheep and pig farmers can get funding for a vet to test animals for diseases and advise on improving animal welfare and productivity.

From: Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs and Rural Payments Agency

Applies to England

What the service is

The service forms part of the Animal Health and Welfare Pathway. It will provide payments to livestock keepers to fund livestock visits by vets.

You can get funding for a vet or team chosen by a vet, to visit your farm if you keep:

  • beef cattle
  • dairy cattle
  • sheep
  • pigs

Animal health and welfare review

There are two parts to the service. The first part is the review.

A review will help you:

  • test for specific endemic diseases in beef cattle, dairy cattle and pigs
  • test the effectiveness of medicines to treat worms in sheep
  • increase livestock productivity
  • improve animal welfare

After the vet has completed a review, you can choose to have an endemic disease follow-up. The vet will test for diseases on the same species as your review.

Endemic disease follow-up

The second part is the endemic disease follow up; this must follow a review.

! Warning All your reviews and follow-ups must be done on the same species tested during your first review.

A follow-up will help you:

  • understand levels of diseases and syndromes on your farm through targeted testing
  • reduce disease levels
  • improve disease and health status through vet advice and a biosecurity assessment
  • increase livestock productivity
  • improve animal welfare

You can choose the vet you want to do the review or follow-up. The review and follow-up are not an inspection or audit.

This is a multiannual agreement which will allow you to:

  • have up to 3 reviews over the course of your agreement, for one livestock type, every 10 months
  • then have up to 3 follow-ups on the same herd or flock within 10 months of the review

Check you’re eligible to apply

To apply, you must have a minimum number of animals present when the vet visits your farm for a review or follow-up.

You must have one of the following:

  • 11 or more beef cattle
  • 11 or more dairy cattle
  • 21 or more sheep
  • 51 or more pigs

The animals can be any age.

The livestock must be registered in England. You must be responsible for their welfare when the vet does a review or follow-up. You can apply at any time of the year.

Your business must have a single business identifier (SBI) linked to a county parish holding (CPH) number. The CPH number must be registered in England.

The CPH number cannot be:

  • an 8,000 number used for abattoirs, pet food manufacturers, racing pigeon establishments and showgrounds
  • a 9,000 number used for poultry keepers

If you got a lump sum payment to leave or retire from farming, you can still apply for funding.

Timing of reviews and follow-ups

There must be:

  • at least 10 months between your reviews
  • at least 10 months between your follow-ups
  • no more than 10 months between your reviews and follow-ups

How much funding you could get

You’ll get funding for each livestock type, not for each animal.

The funding amount for each type of livestock is different because some tests cost more to do.

The funding will contribute to the cost of testing as well as your time and the vet's time.

How it works

  1. Apply and accept your agreement.

  2. Arrange a review and agree payment with the vet.

  3. Claim your funding online.

  4. No more than 10 months later, organise a follow-up for the vet to test for endemic diseases.

  5. Ask your vet to take samples for the follow-up testing.

  6. Arrange for your vet to carry out a biosecurity assessment.

  7. Submit your results when you claim your funding.

If you have an existing agreement with the annual health and welfare review, and you’ve already had a review you should start from step 4 in the list and arrange a follow-up.

You must agree with the vet what they’ll focus on. Ask the vet to read the guidance on how to do a review or follow-up. This is important because the vet needs to follow the guidance closely so that you are eligible to claim.
